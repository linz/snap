New Zealand Geodetic Datum 2000

Description

The NZGD2000 coordinate systems defines coordinates in terms of latitude and longitude in the NZGD2000 datum.

Note that the NZGD2000 datum and coordinate system is for practical purposes the same as the WGS84 datum and coordinate system used by many GPS systems.

Entering coordinates

Coordinates can be entered in either order, with the latitude first or the longitude first. Each coordinate may be entred as either decimal degrees (eg 45.123), degrees and minutes (45 07.38) or degrees minutes and seconds (45 07 23), though both coordinates must be in the same format. Latitudes can optionally be followed by S (for south), and longitudes by E (for east). The coordinates can be separated by space or a comma.

Examples would be "40.123S,171.08E", "171.08 40.123", and "171 05 23 S 40 22 08.1E".
